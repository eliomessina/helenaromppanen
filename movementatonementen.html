MOVEMENTATONEMENT

dance-and-movement therapy

MOVEMENTATONEMENT Movement is naturally inherent in us, in our breath, in the pulsations of our heart, in the fluid connectedness of the tissues throughout all our body. Movement is that creative expression and force of life that enables transformation.
Change, learning and transformation occur through adjustments to life and through the fundamental resonance of the whole organism in relation with its felt purpose and how it's linked to the environment.

That resonance reflects our underlying ability to atone and restore our natural state of being within those changes.

Stillness and silence create a transient space that lies underneath all that movement resonance: in the space between each heartbeat, in the transition from exhalation into inhalation. Thus the essential meaning of atonement is at-one-ment, to be at one, in silence, to be at peace within ones authentic being.

Dance and movement that is practiced through somatic and framework, gives space for authentic self expression to emerge as an experience of holistic body-mind connectivity. This connectivity facilitates improved self image, creativity, confidence and ability to enjoy life which will tone the whole physical appearance as well.

Life is expressed as motion and change. As we approach our moving dancing body-minds, we aim to attune and give space to utter openness to life and it's manifestations.

MOVEMENTATONEMENT IS A MINDFULNES BASED CREATIVE DANCE AND MOVEMENT PRACTISE that promotes well-being, vitality, change and awareness through mindful, psychosomatic body-mind connectivity.

The practices of movementatonement include movement and dance as well as physical exercises which encourage the development of strength, flexibility, breathing techniques, creativity, meditation and relaxation.

The domains of the therapeutic work span motoric, psychosocial and cognitive functions, including specific psychosocial issues related to attunement, attachment, authentic selfrelatedness and empathy.

The creative-therapeutic aspects of the work focus on and re-create sensitivity towards the movements of the body-mind entity, that spontaneously resonates with the deep neutral space of being within other people and within the living world of Nature around us.

During these sessions we aim to make an inquiry towards our own moving inner self space, witnessing with compassion our state of being and the emotions that arise from our individually experienced movement vocabulary and narratives. During the sessions we approach these narratives from various perspectives: Dance and movement improvised in moment, Relaxation, Mindful Awareness practices, Somatic practices and Movement Meditation practice.

Furthermore we explore a range of different practices including breath, posture, gesture, pedestrian and rhythmical movement and also more technical or style-specific form of dancing or other traditional movement meditation (MM) practices.

"With this openness and respect towards a variety of mental-physical disciplines and traditions cultivated both in modern western and ancient eastern culture, the purpose is to explore and integrate the relationships of the body, mind and surroundings and give space for a working atmosphere of unconditional self-acceptance that in itself carries the openness of peaceful and creative inner body-mind space. In this context, afforded by constant “emptying” and “filling” of the body-mind continuum with thoughts, feelings, sensations and movements lies a potentiality and freedom to atone, discover, unfold and change aspects of oneself, as a series of transformative energetic shifts will be naturally activated. The focus on unconditional self-acceptance during the practice will contribute to create an integration in the mind-body entity as a mutually influencing unit, enhance the sense of being present and the body-mind ability to relate itself to the physical world with more flexibility and ability to enjoy life and its manifestations”.
